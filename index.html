Building House of Islam in the hearts of Japan

"Shizuoka Masjid" project is not only a religious center, but also a cultural, educational, and social hub that will benefit both the Muslim community and the Japanese society.

The Masjid will become a platform to create social impact in its society and to showcase the unique fusion of the beauty of Islam and Japanese arts & technologies.

UNIQUENESS

  • Mt.Fuji

    The symbolic icon of Japan will become the iconic view from the Masjid

  • Environmental Impact

  • Muslim Youth Center

    Offering attractive programs and facilities to get youth more involved with Masjid

  • Islamic Cultural Heritage

    Exhibitions, festivals, lectures & library will be creating the most attractive gate for Japanese public to reach JAPAN DAHWA FOUNDATION

Objective

Building

To provide a place of worship for Muslims in Shizuoka city and nearby areas with a Muslim population of 3,000 and more.

Education

To offer educational and social environment where Muslims and non-Muslims can learn and interact through Qur'an/Arabic classes, charity, sports, arts and cultural events.

Safe Place

To become a safe place of community where people can share the message of Islam, promote interfaith dialogue, and contribute to the peace and harmony of the society.

Management

A board of trustees will be elected by the members of the Masjid and will be responsible for the overall direction and supervision of the Masjid. The board will appoint a director who will oversee the administration of the Masjid and staffs including an Imam, teachers, coordinators, a secretary and a cleaner. Volunteers are expected to play great roles in various aspects and activities of the Masjid.

Impacts

1. For Muslims

The essential place where they can practice their faith, enhance their knowledge, strengthen their bonds, and express their identity.

2. For Non-Muslims

The unique place where they can learn about Islam, appreciate its diversity, experience its culture, and interact with its followers.

3. For the Society (long term impacts)

  • To increase the awareness, understanding, and respect for Islam and Muslims among the Japanese people and the wider public.
  • To improve the integration, participation, and representation of Muslims in the Japanese society and the civic life.
  • To foster the development, cooperation, and solidarity of the Muslim community in Japan and the region.
